Challenge

Replacing a manual process with an intelligent first point of contact

The Learning Design team at New Zealand Customs Service used a manual form to handle course page requests. It was a slow, inconsistent process with too much back-and-forth. With AI tools available in Microsoft 365, there was a clear opportunity to streamline it.

Approach

Self-driven, end-to-end - from research to working product

Self-initiated and end-to-end. I identified the opportunity, ran the research, and designed and built the solution independently, spanning UX, conversational flow, Power Automate integration, and instructional design.

Research

Needs analysis with the full team

I spoke with every team member to surface pain points in the existing process. The key issues were:

  • The current process was too time-consuming
  • Communication spread across multiple emails led to unclear expectations from the client
  • Documentation was often inconsistent

Problem Framing and Development

A clear design question, and three rounds of testing

From that research I framed a How Might We question to guide the design direction:

How might we create an agent that automates new course page requests and minimises manual labour?

Once a working prototype was in place, I ran three rounds of usability testing with five participants each. The sessions focused primarily on information architecture and structure, making sure the conversation flow felt logical, that questions appeared in the right order, and that the language matched how the team actually talked about their work.

Solution

An AI agent that guides clients through the request process

Built in Microsoft Copilot Studio, the agent serves as an intelligent intake form. It guides clients through a structured conversation - gathering all the information the Learning Design team needs to begin work on a new course page - then automatically compiles the responses and sends a formatted summary to the team via Outlook.

The back end connects Copilot Studio topics and triggers to a Power Automate flow, which processes the collected answers using dynamic text and routes the completed request to the appropriate inbox - no manual chasing required.

Impact

Pioneering AI adoption
across the organisation

A traditional course request takes around 12 minutes to process, not counting follow-up emails. With 31 requests a year, that's over 6 hours of admin time. The agent handles the same task in roughly 24 seconds, bringing the annual total down to about 12 minutes.

First of its kind

This was the first AI agent deployed within the organisation's Microsoft environment, demonstrating a practical, low-risk application of emerging tools to a real team problem.

Knowledge transfer

To ensure the work had lasting value, I documented the entire build process and created cheatsheets and instructional videos so other staff could confidently build their own agents.

Streamlined intake

The agent replaced an inconsistent manual form, ensuring every request arrives with the right information - reducing turnaround time and freeing up the team administrator to focus on higher-value work.
